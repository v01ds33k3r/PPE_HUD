\section{Potential Usage Scenarios}

Working time in full-body suit with self-contained breathing apparatus
- usually limited to two hours at a time OR shorter, depending on air and exhaustion levels \parencite{}
- exhaustion more extreme with more equipment weight + heat \parencite{}
Depending on configuration, the SCBA can add around 15 kilograms of additional weight.

Additionally, Type 1 suits are not gas-permeable, compromising the body's ability to regulate temperature and leading to even faster exhaustion and heat fatigue \parencite{}. Indicators that are measurable in regular work conditions for catching heat exhaustion early include heart rate (variability), blood pressure \parencite{}.

Hazards are mainly electricity + possible explosive atmospheres
- elimination not in task scope, but to be avoided

limited FOV and mobility
Work conditions/tasks are characterized by a high mental load \parencite{}.
-both make it hard to keep an eye on all of these things

All these attributes can be summarized into two extremes. First, a controlled environment with either bright or poor lighting and simple backgrounds. Second, a scenario like an emergency response, in which most variables are unknown. It is likely that backgrounds will be varied and lighting conditions can change.

The types of information needed would differ by task, but life-sustaining paramaters should be front and center in all of them.
