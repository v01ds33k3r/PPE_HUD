\section{Conclusion}

This project demonstrates the successful development of UI concepts for potential AR technologies intended for use in personal protective equipment, including a detailed presentation of sample components.

The provided design guidelines offer a collection of relevant concepts intended to serve as a solid foundation for exploring further industrial or safety-critical applications that could benefit from head-mounted HUDs. This reinforces the classification of 2D HUDs as augmented reality, as they serve as tools for applications that cannot be covered by world-stabilized systems.

The next step in further developing this topic is the creation of a prototype to evaluate the presented concepts.
Technological limitations of OHMDs could be circumvented by testing aspects of the interfaces using video see-through systems. This would make it possible to test interaction concepts—such as the head-glance interface—as well as animations for different notification levels.

Furthermore, multimodality could be explored, as audio in particular could significantly enhance many of the concepts.
Progress toward a completely hands-free system should also not be overlooked.
