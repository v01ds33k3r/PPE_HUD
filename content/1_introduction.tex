\section{Introduction}

\subsection{Motivation}

While working in personal protection equipment, it is essential to be able to parse critical information, such as oxygen levels, at a glance. Chemical protective suits in particular reduce peripheral vision and mobility \parencite{murrayEffectsChemicalProtective2011}, making it challenging to operate radio devices and other tools.

A possible solution might be a head-up display (HUD), since it could integrate information into the field of view. Numerous studies confirm an increase in task performance when using a HUD (\cite{chauvinAugmentedRealityHUD2023}; \cite{kimInfluencesAugmentedReality2019}).

This thesis aims to analyze requirements and current technological issues, as well as propose potential user interface designs for HUDs independent of hardware implementation.
